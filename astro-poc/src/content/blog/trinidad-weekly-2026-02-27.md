## Executive Summary

Trinidad recorded 52 criminal incidents during the week of February 21-27, 2026, representing a 13% increase from the previous week's 46 incidents. Robbery emerged as the dominant crime type, accounting for nearly one-third of all incidents, while theft cases surged dramatically with a 160% spike. The data reflects a shift in crime patterns, with violent crimes like shootings declining while property crimes accelerated across multiple regions.

## Key Statistics

- **Total Incidents:** 52 (↑ 13% from last week's 46)
- **Total Victims:** 62 across configured crime categories
- **Murder:** 3 incidents (→ no change from last week)
- **Robbery:** 17 incidents (↑ 21% from last week)
- **Theft:** 13 incidents (↑ 160% from last week)
- **Assault:** 13 incidents (↓ 7% from last week)
- **Shooting:** 10 incidents (↓ 29% from last week)

## Regional Breakdown

### San Juan
San Juan recorded the highest incident count with 9 cases and 9 victims. Robbery dominated the area's crime profile with 3 incidents, indicating organized property crime activity concentrated in this region.

### Port of Spain
The capital region reported 5 incidents affecting 6 victims. Robbery remained the primary concern with 2 incidents, suggesting continued vulnerability in commercial and public spaces.

### Arima
Arima documented 4 incidents with 4 victims, marking a shift toward property crimes. Theft accounted for 2 incidents, reflecting the broader trend of increased larceny across the island.

### St Charles
This area reported 2 incidents but 3 victims, indicating higher-impact crimes. A shooting incident on February 23 resulted in 2 casualties, highlighting concentrated violent crime risk.

### Point Fortin
Point Fortin recorded 2 incidents with 2 victims, with theft being the dominant crime type, consistent with the week's property crime surge.

## Crime Type Analysis

### Robbery
Robbery cases increased 21% week-over-week, rising from 14 to 17 incidents. This crime type now represents the largest share of weekly incidents (33%), with concentrated activity in San Juan and Port of Spain. The sustained elevation suggests organized theft targeting commercial establishments and individuals.

### Theft
Theft cases experienced the most dramatic change, jumping 160% from 5 incidents last week to 13 this week. This surge indicates a significant shift toward opportunistic property crimes across diverse locations including Longdenville, Union Village, Diego Martin, Arouca, and San Rafael. The geographic spread suggests either coordinated activity or widespread vulnerability in residential and commercial areas.

### Shooting and Violent Crime Trends
Shooting incidents declined 29% (from 14 to 10), and assault cases fell 7% (from 14 to 13), indicating a modest improvement in violent crime metrics. However, the 3 murders maintained last week's level, suggesting homicides remain a persistent concern despite improvements in other violent categories.

### Notable Decreases
Sexual assault cases dropped to zero this week (from 4 incidents), burglary fell to zero (from 3 incidents), and home invasions declined 50% (from 6 to 3 incidents). These reductions suggest potential effectiveness in targeted prevention efforts or temporary fluctuations in criminal activity patterns.

## Trends and Insights

1. **Property Crime Surge:** The 160% spike in theft cases represents the week's most significant trend, indicating a shift from violent to acquisitive crimes. This pattern may reflect seasonal factors, economic pressures, or reduced enforcement visibility in certain areas.

2. **Robbery Concentration:** Robbery's 21% increase and dominance in San Juan and Port of Spain suggests organized criminal networks targeting high-traffic commercial zones. These areas warrant enhanced patrol presence and community awareness initiatives.

3. **Violent Crime Stabilization:** While shootings and assaults declined, the stable murder rate indicates that the most serious crimes remain unabated. The 3 homicides this week maintain consistency with previous weeks, suggesting entrenched violent crime dynamics.

4. **Geographic Vulnerability:** San Juan's 9 incidents (17% of weekly total) identifies it as the primary hotspot, while the geographic spread of theft cases across 5+ distinct locations indicates island-wide property crime pressure.

## Safety Recommendations

- **Residents:** Increase vigilance in San Juan and Port of Spain, particularly regarding robbery risk. Avoid displaying valuables in public spaces and vary daily routines to reduce predictability.
- **Business Owners:** Implement enhanced security measures in commercial districts, particularly in San Juan where robbery incidents are concentrated. Consider increased surveillance and security personnel during peak hours.
- **Vehicle Owners:** Given the theft surge, park vehicles in well-lit, populated areas and avoid leaving valuables visible. Use steering wheel locks and GPS tracking devices where possible.
- **Community Groups:** Organize neighborhood watch programs in high-incident areas, particularly focusing on theft prevention in residential zones experiencing the recent surge.
- **Travelers:** Exercise heightened caution in Port of Spain and San Juan, especially during evening hours when robbery risk peaks.
