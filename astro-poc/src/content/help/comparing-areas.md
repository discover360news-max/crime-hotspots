## Comparing Areas

The Compare page lets you place two areas side-by-side to directly compare their crime profiles. This is useful for residents deciding between neighbourhoods, journalists covering relative crime rates, or researchers studying hotspot dynamics.

## How to open the Compare page

- Go to [Trinidad Compare](/trinidad/compare/) directly
- From any area profile page, tap the **Compare** button
- From the Areas page, select two areas and tap Compare

## Selecting areas to compare

Use the two dropdown selectors at the top of the Compare page to choose your areas. You can search by name within each dropdown. Once both areas are selected, the page updates automatically.

## What the comparison shows

Each area panel displays:

- **Risk score** (1–10)
- **Total incidents** in the selected time window
- **Crime type breakdown**: a ranked list of the most common offences
- **90-day trend**: whether crime is increasing, decreasing, or stable compared to the previous 90-day period
- **Most recent incident**: the latest verified report from that area

## Time window

The comparison defaults to the past 90 days. You can switch to year-to-date or full year view using the selector above the comparison panels.

## Interpreting the comparison

A higher risk score and higher incident count both indicate more reported crime — but they measure different things:

- **Incident count** is the raw total
- **Risk score** is relative to the national average (see [Understanding Risk Scores](/help/understanding-risk-scores/))

An area with a high incident count but a moderate risk score may simply be a larger area with proportionally average crime. Look at both metrics together for a complete picture.

## Limitations

The comparison only includes media-reported incidents. Areas with lower media coverage may appear safer than they are. See [Data Coverage and Limitations](/help/data-coverage-limitations/) for more.
