## Trend Indicators

Trend indicators appear on area profile pages, the Statistics page, and the Compare page. They show directional movement in crime over time.

## How trends are calculated

Each trend indicator compares two equivalent time windows:

- **Current window**: the most recent 30 days (or 90 days, depending on context)
- **Previous window**: the equivalent period immediately before the current window

If the current window has more incidents than the previous window, the trend is **up**. If fewer, it is **down**. If within a small margin (±5%), it is **stable**.

## What the indicators mean

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Up arrow (red) | Incidents have increased compared to the prior period |
| Down arrow (green) | Incidents have decreased compared to the prior period |
| Dash (neutral) | Stable — within the ±5% threshold |

## On area profile pages

Each area profile shows a 90-day trend indicator for the area's overall crime rate. A red upward arrow means crime in that area has increased over the past 90 days relative to the 90 days before that.

## On the Statistics page

The Statistics page shows trend indicators by crime type — so you can see whether Robberies are trending up nationally even if overall crime is down.

## Interpreting trends carefully

- A single-month spike may not represent a meaningful trend. Use 90-day trends for more reliable signals.
- Low-volume areas can show large percentage swings from small absolute changes (e.g. going from 1 to 2 incidents = +100%).
- Trends reflect reported crime. An increase in trend may indicate more crime, more reporting, or more media coverage of that area.
