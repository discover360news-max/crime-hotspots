Police confrontations on public roads — including traffic stops where suspects suddenly flee or discharge weapons — can occur without warning in any lane of traffic. When a driver accelerates away from a traffic stop and discharges a firearm, bystanders in nearby vehicles or on foot are exposed to stray projectiles, erratic vehicle movements, and the general disorder of an active pursuit. Most civilians have no warning that a routine traffic stop is about to escalate, and the instinct to slow down and observe places you directly in the danger zone. The correct response is to move away from the scene quickly and deliberately rather than stopping to watch.

**Steps to follow:**

- If you witness a vehicle suddenly accelerate away from a police stop or hear gunfire on a public road, do not slow down to observe — drive away from the incident if it is safe to do so, or pull off the road and stay low inside your vehicle.
- Keep your head and body below the window line if you cannot immediately drive away — vehicle doors offer limited but meaningful protection from stray projectiles.
- Do not follow or drive behind the fleeing vehicle even at a distance — you risk entering an active confrontation zone.
- Once you are safely clear of the scene, call 999 and report what you witnessed — note the vehicle colour, direction of travel, and number of occupants if you were able to observe safely.
- Do not post live video or commentary on social media while the incident is ongoing — this can compromise active police operations.
- If your vehicle or a passenger is struck, call 999 immediately and remain inside the vehicle until instructed otherwise by police.
