Standing alone at a street corner waiting for a taxi or maxi is one of the most predictable and exposed positions a person can be in on a public road. You are stationary — your movement is limited — and in many cases visibly holding or wearing something worth taking. In the incident that prompted this tip, a man waiting at the corner of Woodford Street and Tragarete Road in Port of Spain was approached, had his side bag grabbed, and the suspect fled after a brief struggle. The bag contained cash, house keys, chargers, and personal items. A snatch-and-run requires only seconds; the criminal has already assessed whether a struggle is worth the risk before they move. Standing at a known taxi pick-up point in the late afternoon or evening significantly increases your exposure to this method, particularly if you are facing away from the road or distracted by your phone.

**Steps to follow:**

- Wear cross-body bags or position shoulder bags under your arm against your body — a bag hanging freely on your shoulder or at your side is accessible to anyone passing at speed.
- Do not carry everything of value in a single bag; distribute cash, keys, and valuables across your person so that a single snatch does not result in total loss.
- While waiting for transport, position yourself with your back to a wall, building, or fence rather than standing in the open; this removes one angle of approach and forces anyone targeting you to come from the front.
- Limit phone use and keep your head up while waiting; distraction is the primary factor that allows a snatch to succeed without resistance.
- If you notice someone who appears to be watching or circling nearby, move to a busier section of the pavement, enter a nearby business, or call your transportation to confirm their route and ETA as a reason to stay engaged with your surroundings.
- Avoid routinely waiting at the same corner at the same time each day with the same visible bag; predictable behaviour reduces the effort required to target you.
