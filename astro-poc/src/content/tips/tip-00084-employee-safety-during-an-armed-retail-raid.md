When armed individuals storm a retail premises, their first objective is control — and in documented incidents across Trinidad, that control is established through pre-emptive physical violence against staff, regardless of whether employees are resisting. At Mega Savings Supermarket in St. Augustine in March 2026, three armed suspects stole approximately $12,000 in cash and a quantity of cigarettes. During the robbery, one female employee was struck repeatedly on the head with a gun butt. She had not physically resisted. The violence was not a response to resistance — it was a technique used to establish dominance and suppress any attempt to reach an alarm or phone. Understanding this pattern is critical: full and visible compliance remains the strategy most likely to limit harm, because the less reason an attacker has to use additional force, the less likely they are to do so. The more clearly a staff member demonstrates non-resistance — through stillness, silence, and visible open hands — the lower the operational justification for continued violence.

**Steps to follow:**

- In the event of an armed robbery at your workplace, stop moving immediately and place your hands where they can be clearly seen — slow, deliberate, visible movements signal compliance and reduce the chance of being struck.
- Do not attempt to reach for a phone, panic button, or alarm once armed suspects are inside the premises and in direct control of the scene; your physical safety takes priority over triggering a response.
- If a silent alarm was already triggered before suspects gained full control of the scene, do not indicate this — continue to comply fully and let the alarm work without drawing attention to it.
- Avoid sudden movements, reaching into bags or drawers, or turning away from suspects without instruction — uninterpretable movement is frequently met with an immediate physical threat.
- Memorise what you can about the suspects — clothing colours, build, height, whether they were masked, and the direction they exited — without making it obvious that you are studying them; this information is critical for your police report.
- As soon as suspects have left the premises and it is safe to do so, call 999 immediately; do not allow any person to re-enter the scene before police arrive so that the space is preserved as evidence.
