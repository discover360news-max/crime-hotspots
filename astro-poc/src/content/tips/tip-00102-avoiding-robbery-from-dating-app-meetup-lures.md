Dating and social apps are increasingly used by criminals to arrange a meeting under the pretence of a romantic or social encounter and then ambush the victim at a location the criminal controls. The chosen venue is typically a private apartment, compound, or building the victim has no prior knowledge of — a location selected precisely because it is isolated, unfamiliar, and offers no witnesses. In a 2026 incident in Port of Spain, a 34-year-old man was lured via Tinder to an apartment complex on Nelson Street and ambushed from behind by two men who demanded his phone, wallet, watch, and bank card PIN. The victim had no reason to suspect the meeting was staged because the communication beforehand appeared genuine; the danger was in the meeting location, not in the person's online profile.

**Steps to follow:**

- Never agree to meet someone from a dating or social app at their home, apartment, or any private indoor location on a first meeting — insist on a busy public place such as a café, mall food court, or restaurant where other people are present.
- Before leaving for any app-arranged meeting, tell a trusted person the full address, the name or username of the person you are meeting, and a time by which you will check in; do not proceed without sharing this information.
- If the person changes the agreed meeting location to somewhere less public — especially at short notice — treat this as a warning sign and decline; reschedule only to a confirmed public venue.
- When you arrive at an unfamiliar location, pause outside before entering; if the area feels isolated, the entrance is poorly lit, or the building does not match a standard residential or commercial space, leave before going in.
- Trust your instincts at the door — if someone you do not know opens a gate or door and invites you into a space you cannot easily exit, you are not obligated to enter; it is always acceptable to stop and leave.
- If you are robbed, comply with demands and do not resist; once safe, report to the nearest police station with the person's app profile details, username, and any messages exchanged — do not delete the conversation.
