Thieves targeting parked vehicles for parts — wheels, catalytic converters, mirrors, batteries, and other components — operate quickly and in teams, often during early morning hours when residential streets are quiet. A 2026 incident at Webb Trace in Cunupia captured on video surveillance shows two occupants of a Nissan Almera stripping parts from a parked vehicle on a residential road. The footage demonstrates that video surveillance can document the crime and the vehicle used, but does not on its own stop the theft from occurring. Parts theft is planned, not impulsive: suspects target specific vehicle models known to carry resaleable components and return to areas where security is perceived to be low. A vehicle parked in the same unprotected location every night is a consistent, predictable target.

**Steps to follow:**

- Park inside a secured compound or garage overnight wherever possible; a vehicle on an open street with no physical barrier is accessible to any passerby at any hour.
- Install CCTV with coverage of your parked vehicle and ensure the footage is stored off-site or in cloud storage — on-premises DVRs can be taken or destroyed by the same persons committing the theft.
- Mount cameras at angles that capture vehicle registration plates and faces at the approach to the parking area, not only a wide-angle view of the street.
- Vary your parking position if you use street parking; returning to the exact same spot every night removes the uncertainty that deters casual opportunists and organized teams alike.
- Fit locking wheel nuts if your vehicle's rims are a likely target, and consider a catalytic converter shield or cable lock for vehicles with accessible undercarriage components.
- If your vehicle has been targeted or you notice strangers surveying parked cars on your street, report it to police immediately and alert neighbours — parts theft in residential areas often involves the same group returning multiple times.
