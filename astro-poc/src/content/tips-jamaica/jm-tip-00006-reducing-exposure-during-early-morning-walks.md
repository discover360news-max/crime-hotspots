Shootings in Kingston's inner-city communities do not only claim intended targets — anyone visible and on foot in an open area when gunmen act is at risk. In a March 2026 incident, a man walking along Blount Street in Kingston 14 at 5 a.m. was approached and shot at by two armed men who then fled on a motorcycle. The victim escaped unhurt, but the incident illustrates how pre-dawn hours carry elevated risk in high-activity communities: foot traffic is minimal, witnesses are absent, and emergency response is slower. Being outdoors during these hours in areas with documented criminal activity means your proximity to open roadside space — not your involvement in any dispute — is the primary exposure factor.

**Steps to follow:**

- In communities with known criminal activity, avoid walking alone in the early morning hours — particularly between midnight and 6 a.m. — when foot traffic is low and gunmen operate with reduced witness risk.
- If your work or schedule requires pre-dawn travel in areas like Kingston 14, Denham Town, Arnett Gardens, or similar communities, arrange a vehicle or travel with others wherever possible — walking alone along open streets is the highest-risk configuration.
- Do not stand or wait in open roadside positions during early morning hours; if you must wait for transport, wait in a doorway, behind a wall, or in another position that removes you from the open street.
- If you hear gunshots while outdoors, immediately move behind solid cover — a concrete wall, a building corner — and do not return to the open until the area is confirmed clear.
- Report all shooting incidents to 119 even when no one is visibly injured; consistent reporting from residents in Kingston parishes builds the incident data JCF uses for targeted patrols.
- If you believe you may be targeted or have received threats, report the matter to your nearest JCF station before an incident occurs — do not wait until you are approached.
