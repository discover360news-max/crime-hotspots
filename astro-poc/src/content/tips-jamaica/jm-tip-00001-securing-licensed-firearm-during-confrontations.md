A licensed firearm kept within reach during a heated argument can be turned against you or used to harm your property — and the legal consequences fall on both parties. In a February 2026 incident in Goshen, St Elizabeth, a contractor involved in a dispute with a woman seized her licensed firearm and discharged several rounds at her vehicle, damaging the tyres. The weapon was accessible during the confrontation, giving the aggressor an opportunity he would not otherwise have had. When emotions are elevated, reaction time narrows and the presence of a firearm escalates any dispute to lethal potential, regardless of who the registered holder is. Your licence grants you the right to possess the weapon — it does not guarantee you will retain physical control of it during a physical altercation.

**Steps to follow:**

- Store your licensed firearm in a secure, locked location when you are at home — a dedicated safe or lockbox, not a bedside drawer or accessible shelf where it can be grabbed during a confrontation.
- If you are in a dispute with someone inside your home or on your property, do not retrieve your firearm unless you face an immediate, credible threat to your life — drawing it during an argument creates the exact opportunity for it to be taken.
- If you anticipate a confrontation with someone who may become physically aggressive, contact the JCF before the meeting and remove your firearm from the accessible area of your home first.
- Secure your firearm before allowing contractors, workers, or anyone unfamiliar into your home — the presence of others you do not fully trust is itself a reason to lock it away.
- Report any theft or seizure of your licensed firearm to the JCF immediately — delay in reporting worsens your legal exposure.
